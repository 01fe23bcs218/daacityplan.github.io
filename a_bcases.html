Target 11.2: By 2030, provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety, notably by expanding public transport, with special attention to the needs of those in vulnerable situations, women, children, persons with disabilities, and older persons.

Example Scenario: Indore TNC Optimization

A Transportation Network Company (TNC) in Indore aims to optimize routes for drivers using real-time traffic and ride demand data. The system calculates the shortest travel time between various zones in the city to ensure efficient and timely rides.

Office Hubs and Pickup Points Across Indore:

Sl. No. Zone Name Number of Pickup Points
1 Vijay Nagar 12
2 Rajwada 8
3 Bhanwarkuan 10
4 Palasia 6
5 Dewas Naka 9

Impact of Zone-Wise Optimization

By analyzing the distribution of pickup points and leveraging real-time traffic data, the TNC can optimize the route and allocation process to improve efficiency and reduce travel times. This approach ensures timely rides for passengers and better resource management for drivers.

Algorithms Used

  • Dijkstra's Algorithm:
    • Used for finding the shortest path between a source and all other nodes in the graph.
    • Implements a priority queue to calculate the shortest travel time considering the traffic conditions stored in uber_routes_input.txt.
    • Outputs the shortest distance to all zones in the city from a source node.
  • Kruskal's Algorithm:
    • Applied for generating a Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) to optimize the network connectivity between zones and reduce overall travel costs.
  • Merge Sort and Binary Search:
    • Merge Sort: Optimizes the ordering of customer requests for efficient processing.
    • Binary Search: Locates specific drivers or zones efficiently in sorted arrays of requests.

Scenario: ISP Expansion in Indore

An Internet Service Provider (ISP) is expanding its high-speed fiber network across Indore. To meet increasing data demand, the ISP must optimize its fiber installation network to minimize costs while ensuring reliable connectivity across key zones. The use of Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithms helps in achieving this objective efficiently.

Zones and Data Demand:

Sl. No. Zone Name Data Demand (MBps)
1 Old Palasia 300
2 Bhawarkua 450
3 Annapurna 350
4 Rau 250
5 Kanadia Road 200

Algorithms Used

  • Prim's Algorithm:
    • Computes the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) to optimize cable distribution.
    • Outputs the edges and weights that form the minimum network cost.
  • Floyd-Warshall Algorithm:
    • Computes the shortest paths between all pairs of nodes to assess network latency and optimize redundancy in connectivity.
  • Bellman-Ford Algorithm:
    • Detects negative weight cycles in the graph to ensure network reliability.
    • Validates that all shortest paths computed are feasible in the system.
  • Selection Sort and Linear Search:
    • Selection Sort: Organizes customer bandwidth requests for efficient handling.
    • Linear Search: Locates specific requests in an unsorted list.

3. E-commerce Warehouse Optimization

Target 11.6: By 2030, reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including by paying special attention to air quality and municipal and other waste management.

Indicator 11.6.1: Proportion of urban solid waste regularly collected and with adequate final discharge out of total urban solid waste generated, by cities.

Scenario: E-commerce Warehouse Optimization in Indore

An e-commerce company operates warehouses in Indore and aims to minimize transportation costs and delivery times. The goods are transported from the warehouses to various zones in the city. This transportation problem is represented using a cost matrix, and the goal is to optimize the flow of goods by selecting the most efficient routes.

Warehouses and Capacities:

Sl. No. Warehouse Name Capacity (in MB)
1 Lasudia Mori 2000
2 Rajendra Nagar 1500
3 Sanwer Road 2500

Algorithms Used

  • Quick Sort:
    • Used to sort product IDs or warehouse data for better prioritization during optimization.
  • DFS and BFS Traversals:
    • DFS (Depth-First Search): Explores all routes within the warehouse layout to analyze connectivity.
    • BFS (Breadth-First Search): Determines shortest paths to optimize internal transportation of goods.
  • Heap Sort and Binary Search:
    • Heap Sort: Prioritizes products based on their importance or demand.
    • Binary Search: Quickly locates a specific product or zone in a sorted list.
